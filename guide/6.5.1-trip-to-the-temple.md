### 6.5.1. Trip to the Temple

> 🥈 **A Path to the Stars** — Recover the Aero-Prism and escape the Soldier's Temple. _(RetroAchievements · 10 pts)_
> 🥉 **Initiate: Positron Bolt** — Recover the Positron Bolt parts in Vahal Fort. (Missed upon finding Elsydeon.) _(RetroAchievements · 5 pts)_
> ⚠ **Missable** — must recover Positron Bolt parts in Vahal Fort BEFORE finding Elsydeon. The sword triggers the cutoff.

```
Starting Party: Wren , Chaz , Rika , Rune
Starting Level: 38-39, 41-42, 43-44, 38
Try to enter the cave and a cut-scene will begin.

A man will be standing at the entrance to the cave, and will ask if the
party are there to inspect the ruins. Chaz will reply that they are (sort
of), and ask the man who he is. He apologises for not saying his name
earlier, and introduces himself as Seth, a travelling archaeologist. He
tells the party that he is looking for the ruins of an ancient temple, but
that monsters inhabit the cave that leads to it. Chaz tells Seth that the
temple is exactly where they're going, and Seth thinks this very fortunate.
Seth requests if he can accompany the party, pointing out that he has
confidence in his skills. Chaz wonders how an archaeologist can be of any
help, but Wren adds that there is strength in numbers. Chaz accepts, and
Seth is extremely grateful. Rika comments to Rune on how nice and polite
Seth is, but Rune's mind seems to be elsewhere.


Seth joins the party (Level 35)

However you play Seth, make sure to put him at the front of the party so
that he absorbs most of the damage.

strength, pointing out that they must have felled many monsters, and Chaz
gets a little embarrassed.

where you should go right. Take the stairs.

Seth marvels at the party's abilities again, wishing that he had that kind
of strength. Chaz explains that it is the result of many hours training,
and tells Seth that he could be that strong if he put the effort into it.
Seth thinks about this, and Rika asks when Chaz ever did any training.

Follow the passage to the right, then turn up at the junction. Cross the
large chamber that follows, and go through the door at the top. You are now
outside at the top of the cave. Seth announces that they are at the temple,
and gives all credit to the party. He says there may still be monsters, and
asks to accompany the party just a little further. Chaz complies.

Remove all the equipment from Seth, as he will have no further use of it,
then head a little left and then up across the plateau. Walk up to enter
the temple at the top, then go around either left or right and up towards
the top of the room. Head down the steps in the middle and stop just before
the chest. Heal the party completely (not Seth), then open the chest. You
will acquire the AERO-PRISM.

Chaz announces his discovery, but asks how it will show the way to Rykros.
Rune replies that he has no idea, and Chaz sarcastically comments that he
is a big help. Walk up the steps and then around to the bottom of the
temple. Equip Wren's best single-target weapon (PLSMLAUNCH or PHOTNERASR),
then walk on out to begin a cut-scene.

The Aero-Prism suddenly becomes intensely radiant. Chaz comments on this,
and Rune tells him to hold the Aero-Prism to the sky. Chaz does this, and
a brilliant beam of light points directly upwards through the clouds. Chaz
asks what's going on, and Rune tells him that Rykros is at the end of the
light. Wren begins to calculate its direction, and Seth suddenly screams in
pain, clutching his face. Rika asks what's wrong, but Seth doesn't seem in
a position to answer. He continues screaming as he seems to be ripped apart
from the inside. A moment later, where Seth stood is now occupied by a
horrifying yet familiar figure. Rika sees that it is Dark Force, and Rune
is shocked to realise that Dark Force had been masquerading as a human.
Wren wonders if Dark Force can also evolve, but there doesn't seem time to
answer this question before the final battle with Dark Force begins.


Seth leaves the party

The final manifestation of Dark Force is far more humanoid than its
predecessors. It is also the most powerful of the three. Even using the
strategy below, you may find this battle considerably difficult, as you
only have four characters available.

In the first round, set up the standard defence by getting Chaz to use his
RAYBLADE, having Rika increase the party's Agility by using SANER, letting
Rune let loose an EFESS, and having Wren set up a BARRIER. Hopefully, Wren
will create the BARRIER before Dark Force uses a damaging Skill, else one
or all of the characters will take a severe beating.

Dark Force has exactly the same Skills that Seth had (except DTHSPELL),
including CORRSION (this hit-all Skill is far more powerful this time),
SHADOWBIND (reducing the Agility of all organic characters) and MINDBLST
(a hit-all Skill that may inflict Sleep on each character). It also has a
normal, fairly damaging, attack.

In the second round, get Chaz to use RAYBLADE again, have Rika use DEBAN,
get Rune to unleash another EFESS, and have Wren just attack normally. If
the party need healing in this round, get Wren to use a STAR-DEW instead of
attacking. If only one character is injured, let him use a TRIMATE or his
RECOVER Skill.

In the following rounds, keep Chaz using RAYBLADE (or NATHU when this runs
out), let Rika take over the healing by using SAR, GISAR or GIRES (or NASAR
or NARES if necessary), have Rune use EFESS (or LEGEON or NAWAT when you
run out), and get Wren to attack or use RECOVER on himself.

As usual, let Rika throw out a DBLSLASH if no healing needs to be done, and
do not use AROWS if any characters fall asleep. After all, it is likely
that any characters that don't wake up immediately will wake up in the next
round. Using AROWS would just be a waste of a chance to use one of Rune's
powerful offensive spells.

If Rika falls asleep and there is healing to be done, get Wren to cover for
her by using a STAR-DEW or a TRIMATE, or let Chaz use one of his curative
Techniques if you are low on items.

Try to keep every characters' HP above half their maximum value at all
times, and if one of your party members is flatlined, follow the standard
fast/slow revival strategy or use a SOL-DEW or a REPAIR-KIT.

A little more care may need to be taken in this battle, as Dark Force's
Sleep-inflicting Skill may take characters out of commission for a round or
two on a fairly regular basis. It may be necessary to postpone attacking
Dark Force for an entire round if healing needs to be done.

This battle will very likely take a lot longer than any of the previous
boss battles, and possibly use a few items in the process, but if you keep
the characters at a safe HP level, it should all be over in around ten to
thirteen rounds.
Dark Force explodes in his usual dramatic way, and you can rest assured
that you won't be meeting him again. Rune thinks the situation is becoming
a bit sticky, and suggests that they go to Rykros as quickly as possible.
Chaz doesn't understand the hurry, but Rune explains that the followers of
Darkness are probably already aware of the location of Rykros through Dark
Force. Wren points out that they only know the direction of Rykros, but he
admits that there must be something there that the Darkness doesn't want
them to have. Chaz says that they had best get it, and Rika adds that they
will have to hurry before they are beaten to it. Wren reports that he has
the direction of Rykros stored in memory, but the distance is undetermined.
Rune proposes that they just head towards it in the Landale, so Chaz
decides to return to the Spaceport.
```
