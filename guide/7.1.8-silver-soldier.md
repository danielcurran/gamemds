### 7.1.8. Silver Soldier

> 🥈 **HG: Silver Soldier** — Complete 'Silver Soldier' in the Hunter's Guild. (Missed upon finding Elsydeon.) _(RetroAchievements · 10 pts)_
> ⚠ **Missable** — must be completed before finding Elsydeon (6.5.7). Late-game Hunter's Guild quest. Available after Dezolis arc.

```
Recommended Level:  36+
When Available:     After obtaining the Hydrofoil
                    Before obtaining Elsydeon
Commission Awarded: 80000 MST
This is a request from an old man in Zema.

"We are having problems with some strange machines that have begun to roam
 the outskirts of the village. We have not yet been attacked, but we
 villagers are afraid and would like something to be done about it."

That is the message.


After accepting this job, ensure that the party are fully healed and then
teleport to Zema. Head inside and you will be accosted by three Servants.
Defeat these guardians, then head to the house in the northeast corner of
the village. Speak to the old man in the room on the right, and he will
explain that, in the last week, unidentified machines have been roaming the
village. It seems like they are trying to investigate Birth Valley. They
are not doing any harm to the villagers, but it is making everyone uneasy.
He requests that the party drive the machines away. Chaz finds it strange
that they were attacked upon entering the village, but Wren points out that
he seemed to be their only target.

You could speak to the villagers to learn more about the problem, but all
you really need to do is go to the source of the machines. Teleport to
Nalya, then get into the Hydrofoil and head due east. Continue onto an
island, and stop outside the metal structure. This is Vahal Fort. Enter it.

Wren will report that there is a transmission from Demi. She will inform
him that the Plate System has sustained an attack from a mysterious android
attack squad. She reports that she has kept the damage to a minimum, but
she judges from the sophistication of their attack that there must be a
sophisticated tactician behind it. Rika can't understand how someone could
still be sending spurious orders now that Kuran is silenced, and Wren
reports to Demi that they are investigating the matter as well. He advises
her to shift to Alert Mode 1. Demi agrees, then cuts out. Rika asks if the
attack squad could be anything like they met in Zema, and Wren replies that
it seems orders are being given for the destruction of all AI systems above
a certain level. Chaz then sees why Wren was attacked in Zema, and Rika
realises that the reason the machines were in Zema in the first place was
to destroy Seed. Rune asks if the person responsible for the attack didn't
know Seed had already self-destructed, and Wren theorizes that someone in
possession of old Parmanian data, and far removed from Zelan, must be
responsible, and Chaz suggests that they could be here.

Head up to the junction, then go right, up and then right, and pick up a
REPAIR-KIT. Return left, down, and then left past the entrance to the left
wall. Continue up, left and then up again, ignoring the alcove on the left,
and head up to the top wall. Go right and then up, right and down the
corridor until you reach a central elevator shaft. Go down around it to the
right, and take the passage to the right from here. Head up the corridor at
the end, then turn right and then down. Pick up the PHOTNERASR from the
bottom left of this chamber, then return up, left, down and left. Head down
and left to the elevator, and descend to the floor below.

Walk straight down the walkway, ignoring the paths to the left and right
for the moment. Continue to the bottom and pick up the LACO-GEAR. Unless
Wren already has the PALMA-RING, equip this new item to him. Return back up
and turn either left or right, and then up at the corner. Keep going up and
then back around to continue up the walkway to the top wall. There are two
ways to go here, but first you should go left. Head left, down, left and
down, and keep going down until you reach the bottom. Walk right, down and
right, then procure the LACO-ARMOR. As before, only equip this to Wren if
he hasn't already acquired the GUARDARMOR.

Return back left, up and then right to the junction, then continue right,
down, right and then down all the way to the bottom. Turn left, down and
left, then take the elevator in the wall above. Upon arrival, Rune will
comment that the place is awesome, and add that if everything here started
to move at once, Motavia would instantly fall under their control. Chaz
sarcastically asks Rune to remind him why they are here, and Wren tells
them that they have to hurry, as it is not a joking matter.

Walk a little down and then left, ignoring the platform above you, and use
the terminal on the left of the walkway. Head back to the right, and step
onto the conveyor belt at the end. On the other side, step onto the next
conveyor above you, then head right over the thin walkway and take the
elevator to the next floor.

Head straight down the corridor to the bottom and open the chest. Chaz asks
if they are more of Wren's spare parts. Wren looks in the chest and states
that he can't believe it, as he thinks it's a Positron Bolt unit. Wren
explains that it is a weapon developed for attacking space fortresses, and
that a 10-second burst could even destroy Zelan. Rune is amazed at the
power of the unit, yet notes how small it is. Wren adds that, under normal
circumstances, he would prefer to avoid using such a weapon, but admits
that in the current situation he has no choice. He installs it.


Wren acquires the POSIBOLT Skill
Go back up the passage and use the elevator. Head left across the walkway,
then use the conveyor belt above you. At the top, walk left all the way to
the end of the walkway, ignoring the platform below, then step onto the
belt at the end. Back on the walkway again, head left and take the elevator
to the bottom floor. Go down to the corner, then left until you emerge in a
larger chamber. Walk up to the panels above, then completely heal your
party. Examine one of the panels.

As Wren is wondering what the system is, it speaks. The computer welcomes
them, and introduces itself as Daughter, the protector of Algo, responsible
for administering law and ensuring order. Rune asks of what it is a
daughter, but Rika seems to know, saying that Seed told her the plan had
been abandoned. Wren confirms that his data says that as well, but admits
that he must have an error in his data. Chaz asks Daughter if it was the
one that sent androids to Zema and the Plate System, and Daughter replies
that he is correct. Chaz then asks why, if Daughter is the protector of
Algo, it would destroy the environmental control system. Daughter explains
that it is trying to purge the AIs not under its control, and that its
primary objective is peace and prosperity for all of Algo. It adds that any
AI that does not obey its orders is an enemy of Algo and must be destroyed.

Rika takes control of the conversation now, asking Daughter if it was aware
that its network is not connected to external systems, and that any order
it did send would not reach the other systems. Daughter disagrees, saying
it was designed to take control of all remaining systems of Mother Brain,
and that all AI systems in Algo are under its control. Wren informs
Daughter that there is a fundamental error in its self-awareness. He tells
it that, because the environmental deterioration caused by the system
crash was greater than initially expected, the effects of the destruction
of Parma were calculated prior to the completion of the Daughter Project.
He goes on to say that the project was then abandoned at the prototype
manufacturing stage, and that Daughter is but a test machine. Daughter
questions this, but Wren continues that, subsequently, a network was
constructed in Zelan whose sole object was to restart and stabilize Algo's
environmental control system. Wren introduces himself as the AI in charge
of managing the entire system, and Daughter replies that it understands.

Daughter and Wren say little for a moment, then Daughter continues by
saying that it understands that Wren is the one creating disorder in its
system. It adds that it will use all its power to exterminate anything that
interferes with its programs, and then a boss battle begins.

Three Dominators are the sum total of Daughter's defences, and it shouldn't
be a great chore to defeat them. Use hit-all Techniques and Skills (try out
Wren's POSIBOLT), and the Dominators should only last a couple of rounds.
After the battle, Wren approaches the computer terminal and tells Daughter
that he is going to shut it down. Daughter asks why Wren is doing it,
telling him that Algo would never retain its former prosperity without it.
Daughter, in desperation, adds that there is even a possibility that all
will be ruined. Wren explains to Daughter that he was only given minimal
environmental control ability, yet humans have continued to live for over a
thousand years. Daughter begs Wren not to shut it down, but Wren continues
by telling it that civilization has indeed fallen into decay, but that this
is exactly why humans have retained their vitality. He tells it that humans
worked together to overcome all of the problems they have faced. Daughter
sounds weak now, and all you can make out is the word 'stop'. Finally, Wren
tells Daughter than there is no need for excessive interference. Daughter
is then shut down.

Wren turns to the party and explains that technicians at the time planned
to reopen the project after the environment was stabilized. He supposes
that they must have had an independent AI at Nurvus that was keeping things
under control at Vahal Fort. He notes that both he and Demi were negligent,
ignoring the ancient facility because it was unrelated to their official
tasks. Rune adds that all efforts were put into starting Zelan, so Daughter
was forgotten. Rika concludes that a signal must have been sent to wake
Daughter after they had shut down Nurvus. 

After this long dialogue, teleport out of Vahal Fort (or walk if you wish),
then get back to the Hunters' Guild to collect your dues.
```
