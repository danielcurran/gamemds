Wren is the manager of Zelan, responsible for controlling the entire Algo
system network. He joins the party to help sort out the problems on Kuran,
and stays with them until the end of the game. His reason for staying with
the party is not an emotional one, as Wren does not possess emotions.
Instead, he seems to be driven by his programming, helping the party until
the Algo system is back to a stable state. As this is his primary function,
he does whatever is necessary to accomplish it.

Like Demi, Wren's past is not revealed in the game, as it involves mainly
routine maintenance and management of the Algo control system on Zelan. He
is an older model than Demi, and although far more advanced intellectually,
he does not possess feelings. To this end, Wren is a useful pillar of logic
and reason for the party, in addition to being an invaluable source of
information. He is also an expert in the use of firearms.

Wren is an enormous asset to the party, as he is capable of piloting the
Landale, driving the vehicles, interacting with other AI systems and other
tasks involving the advanced remnants of the past. He is also equipped with
sensors that allow him to provide information on their surroundings and
other beings they encounter.

Through spending time with the party, Wren tries to understand human
behaviour, and is helped in this endeavour by his pupil, Rika. Of special
interest to him are the disagreements between Chaz and Rune, and the
growing feelings between Chaz and Rika. The things he has learned from the
party are reflected in his conversation with Daughter in Vahal Fort, and
also in his understanding of Rika's decision at the end of the game.


Wren's favoured weapons are guns, and he wears special android armours.

Wren cannot use Techniques, as he is not human.

His Skills include self-regeneration, protection, a beam weapon and causing
instant death, and he can also install new weapons as Skills as well.
