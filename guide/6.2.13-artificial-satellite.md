### 6.2.13. Artificial Satellite

> 🥉 **Wren** — Recruit Wren. _(RetroAchievements · 1 pts)_

```
Starting Party: Chaz , Rika , Rune
Starting Level: 19-20, 19-20, 21-22
Head up the corridor and ascend in the elevator at the top. Turn left and
follow the short passage into a room. Collect the PLSM-SWORD, PLSM-CLAW and
PLSMDAGGER, then head back to the right, around the central column and
through the next passage to acquire the PLSM-FIELD, PULSE-LASR and the
CANCELLER. Equip the PLSM-SWORD to Chaz, and the PLSM-CLAW to Rika to
complete her set. The other items have little use right now.

Head back to the left and then around and up the corridor above the
elevator. Keep going until you reach a tall silver-clothed person. This is
Wren, so talk to him and begin the next cut-scene.

Wren introduces himself to the party. He then goes on to explain that Zelan
controls the environmental systems for all the planets in Algo, and that he
manages the system. He then asks Rika who the rest of the party are. She
introduces Chaz and Rune, telling him they are great friends, but Chaz
seems to disagree with that remark. Rika goes on to say that Demi informed
them that the problem with Motavia's systems was supposed to have been
caused by Zelan. Wren then explains that Zelan is not behind the systems
running out of control, but that Zelan has no control at all. It seems that
Kuran, the support satellite to Zelan, has somehow usurped Zelan's control
and is sending Motavia's systems the abnormal commands. The fact that Zelan
was at the top of the command chain led Demi to believe that it was the
cause, as Zelan's communications had been down for the last six months.
Chaz then suggests they go to Kuran, and Wren asks if he may accompany the
party, as there is nothing that he can do from Zelan. Rika vouches for
Wren, telling Chaz that Wren and Seed were her teachers. After another
little argument between Chaz and Rune, the party decide to leave.


Wren joins the party (Level 20)

Head back down and around the central column to take the elevator to the
floor below. From here, head all the way down the passage and out onto the
shuttle again. Kuran was originally in Stealth Mode, making it undetectable
to radar, but using the CANCELLER they found earlier the party are able to
disable the Stealth Mode and locate Kuran. Select Kuran as the destination,
and the shuttle departs. A cut-scene then follows.

Chaz notices something is wrong, and then an alarm sounds. Wren reports
that there is an unidentified energy reaction coming from the engine room,
so the party decide to investigate. A short while later, and they discover
an uninvited guest playing around with the controls. It didn't expect to be
found out so soon, but it decides that it doesn't matter whether it
sabotages the engines or kills the party immediately, so long as they don't
get to Kuran. The Chaos Sorcerer then initiates a battle.

A break in the pattern of increasing boss difficulty, the ChaosSorcr is
exceptionally easy to defeat. The ChaosSorcr will either use a laser beam
attack, Flaeli, Hewn or ShadowBind (a target-all Skill that has a small
chance of reducing the Agility of each character). Have all the characters
use their standard attacks, and the ChaosSorcr will go down in the second
round, probably only getting the chance to act once in the entire battle.
The saboteur is dead, but it seems he did his work well. The ship is in
trouble, and Wren points out that they are off their intended flight-path.
He suggests that they make a crash landing on Dezolis, and asks the party
for their opinion. Chaz replies, rather alarmingly, that they don't have a
choice, and Wren makes preparations to crash. Rika seems to be enjoying
herself, and Chaz gets panicky, asking if she's thinking straight. Wren and
Rune tell Chaz to calm down, but Chaz can't understand how everyone can be
so relaxed about it all. The ship begins to descend.
```
