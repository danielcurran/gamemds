## 7.2. Megid - The Anger Technique

```
You must have acquired ELSYDEON before you can complete this side mission.


Go to Rykros. Leave the Silence Temple, and head southeast to the Courage
Tower. From here, head due west through the thin passageway until you reach
an area filled with a few green rocks. The largest of these rocks has a
door at the front. Save your game, then enter.

Chaz will notice that Elsydeon is shining, and Rika states that a voice is
reverberating in her head. The voice tells them that the Anger Tower gladly
welcomes the Protectors of Algo.

Head up the corridor, then turn right when you reach the top. Head down at
the corner, then left a little, then continue down to take the stairs at
the bottom. Head up and right to acquire the GUARD-MAIL, which should be
equipped to Chaz immediately. Go back to the stairs, then descend to the
floor below. Return up and left to the junction, then continue left and
down, picking up the SOL-DEW in the middle of the floor. Go to the bottom
right corner and climb the staircase.

Go up to the top, then right to the far wall. Walk down and go left through
the small passage, then head down to the staircase. Take it to the top
floor. You emerge in a large room, in the middle of which is a person who
looks very familiar - Alys.

Chaz is also entirely alone, the rest of the party having been unable to
follow. You can return down the stairs to rejoin them if you wish, but it
would be best to approach the Alys-like figure. Address her, and Chaz will
ask why she is on Rykros. There is no response. Instead, Alys starts to
push Chaz, and a battle follows.

This is not really a boss fight, although the music is different and
ironically appropriate. Just have Chaz use normal attacks, as Alys should
not be able to inflict enough damage to kill him before she falls.
After the battle, Chaz cries out for Alys, and an unseen laughter can be
heard. Chaz asks who it is, and the room disappears into blackness. A ball
of fire appears, and teases Chaz by saying he is a weakling for wailing and
lamenting for something he knows to be an illusion. Chaz is baffled, and
begins to get annoyed. The fire seems to take enjoyment from the anger and
hatred coming from Chaz, and comments that it is quite a show of Chaz's
force of character. It asks Chaz if he would like to be able to transform
those feelings into strength, but Chaz doesn't understand. The fire goes on
to explain that there is a technique to transform emotions of anger and
hatred into powers of destruction. It asks if Chaz wants to know about it.

Whatever you do, select 'No'. If you reply that Chaz does want to know, the
ball of fire will be disappointed with Chaz, and tell him that it finds it
ridiculous that a fellow like Chaz would consider himself worthy to wield
Elsydeon. It will then attack. Re-Faze (as this is its name) is immensely
powerful, and uses a technique called Megid. This inflicts severe damage on
Chaz, and will ultimately kill him. There is no way to win this fight.

After selecting 'No', Chaz will tell Re-Faze not to mess with him, and
Re-Faze will ask why. Chaz explains that winning with such a power would be
meaningless, and would only make them the same as the Darkness. Re-Faze
seems to be impressed, saying that Chaz has fought the illusion yet hasn't
lost sight of himself. It says it will trust Chaz with the forbidden
technique, Megid.

Re-Faze tells Chaz it is necessary to feel anger and hatred for humans to
be human, and he feels that there shouldn't be any danger that Chaz will be
corrupted by the power of Megid as long as he doesn't forget his true self.


Chaz acquires the MEGID Technique
Make your way back to the Silence Temple by teleporting or otherwise, then
head into the Landale and select your next destination.
```
