# 7. Capsule Monsters

Monsters, some of them Kill us, some help us...the ones who scrap with us are

our enemies, however....we have an exception to this rule...you know! the one

where "All" Monsters are "Bad" and the game which strikes to mind is, Lufia II;

hiding within the colourful landscape we call "Lufia World" there are several

"Capsule Monsters" who are here to aid Maxim and Co throughout the adventure!

This section, which involved loads of work is also thanks to Phoenix 1911, he

had some nice content within his guide on feeding monsters, and his FAQ also

gave me some ideas on the stats of the monsters too, useful FAQ to have around

if you don't mind me saying, if you desire an experts opinion, then read the

guide by Phoenix 1911, he has played this game for longer than me, SO! he is

the "BRAINY" one here! The techniques list was all my work, so this should be

a useful source for information while researching Capsule monsters, please sit

**back and enjoy....here goes...**

@ Brief Lecture!

Many of us would be confused at the term "Capsule Monsters" although if you are a Pokémon Fan, then these type of creatures will be familiar to you! With these fiends in your team, you are most likely going to fell better, it's like having a fifth ally, which CANNOT be controlled by YOU! As these creatures level up; techniques will be gained, feeding them will help them grow into larger forms and battle experience will increase their status. Let me introduce the little buggers to you....starting with....

```
@@@@@
JELZE     Neutral (Non-Elemental)
@@@@@
The Normal Standard ball of cotton wool; Jelze is a fairly average capsule
monster with some strong Physical attacks, to begin with, Jelze has a high
Guts rating; so he will run away only on RARE occasions.....his offensive and
defensive stats are good, and he is fair when it comes to magic resistance; I
would have him at a high level; you will get him as a lump of fluff, but when
he reaches his master level, he will become a Gold Fox; which has some really
cool physical attacks...give him a shot on your team, he's worth a go!
```

Location: When you have completed the tasks around Sundletan, enter the small shrine leading to Alunze and move anti-clockwise around the mountain range; keep going and you should find a lone tree, enter this tree; walk over to the water and claim "Jelze" for your collection!

```
@@@@@
BLAZE     Fire
@@@@@
Blaze can engulf his enemies in flames, he is strong in some places and weak
in others, his main weakness is Guts! Blaze will run when things get hard and
dangerous; but he is both offensive Physically and Magically which is always
a Bonus....keep feeding Blaze so he evolves into his awesome "Fire Bird" form
which is real cool! He is quite Defensive and he has average magic resistance
compared to some monsters; he's a good choice however!
```

**Location:** During the tasks of Clamento, enter Ruby Cave and find an armoured

Dog in a hidden place, that's Blaze and he will join the team when you find him! Do NOT miss him..this monster is valuable!

```
@@@@@
GUSTO     Wind
@@@@@
Gusto is capable of travelling the winds with high velocity, he is the Capsule
monster of Wind! His main status would be his Intelligence and Speed, he is the
fastest of all capsule monsters and he has some "Blow Away" attacks...like his
Cyclone wave and Electro Bolt techniques! I would have him around for the quick
battles where speed plays a large role...if you are fighting Daos...Gusto is a
bad choice as he will die within one blow of Daos's "DARK FRY"...However; he is
useful and should not be missed!
```

**Location:** Inside the Tower of Sacrifice, when you get a choice of three doors;

take a peak inside the middle door and Gusto will be waiting for the Pick-Up, he will join and you have him forever!

```
@@@@@
SULLY     Soil (Earth Elemental)
@@@@@
Sully is very much like GUY and DEKAR, he is strong physically and he has the
intelligence of a Decomposed Turnip...however he is an essential monster to be
obtained, you NEED to get hold of him! Sully would be the favourite of everyone
who owns this game, because unlike the others....Sully can take loads of pain
and he never runs without a good reason...he lacks speed and magic resistance
however, which can be a major downside against fast enemies! DO NOT MISS THIS
MONSTER; he is the real deal!
```

**Location:** Flower Mountain, he is fairly hard to miss, and he is well worth the

bother....he is a must find monster and he would be a worthy monster for your collection!

```
@@@@@
ZEPPY     Water
@@@@@
Zeppy lacks speed a little and his other stats like strength and intelligence
are average....he still rules when it comes to Magic Resistance and HP however;
he is a useful monster to have around but he lacks powerful magical techniques;
instead he has a whole hoard of Physical attacks....overall he's a good choice
for your team; you had better keep Zeppy in his Level 4 form so he can use his
Magic attacks, the "Lightning" elemental ones!
```

Location: Some cave with a screwed up name! You will need the submarine and the Warp Spell or item! Cast "Warp" for 8MP and return to Parcelyte, sink underwater and look for a hole in the seabed...now move around until you've reached one of the whirlpools, some contain Fruits and one of them will lead you to ZEPPY! So you now have the RED FISH!

```
@@@@@
FLASH     Light (Sacred Elemental)
@@@@@
The Luminous Angel of monsters, she is Flash...the BRIGHT SPARK...due to her
abnormal intelligence stats. Flash would be one of my favourite monsters, she
can use some awesome healing spells and her physical status gets better when
she eats more...or evolves into her larger forms! She is well worth snagging
as she will play a major role when it comes to healing, she can even surpass
Selan in healing with her higher intelligence! Her HP's are the only problem;
Flash is limited and she's got the least HP of all capsule monsters; but do
not let this fool you, Flash is by far "one of the best" capsule monsters in
the game..so GIVE ER' A TRY!
```

Location: You can get your mitts on Flash if you visit the North Labyrinth during the tasks set within the Bound Kingdom....You Know! The help Prince Alex and Dekar Thing...you will need your hook to reach this monster, when you have her....you should have a suitable familiar for the "White Witch" Selan!

@@@@@

DARBI Dark (Shadow Elemental) @@@@@ Very! Very! Mysterious, I like him, even though he is Gutless...and his Defense would also be bad! He has some negative status techniques....the usual, Confuse and Poison spells, AND, instant death attacks; which are always handy...if you need some monster to have single round battles with your enemies then Darbi is the ideal choice; he is still lame and he has poor Magic Resistance...including his Guts; but if you need a tough o' darkness to kill your foes; call Darbi's number and watch him in action!

**Location:** Darbi is located in a small shrine on "Crescent island" so finding

him should not be too much of a problem, when you get your ship he is accessible; so don't hold back when it comes to locating him!

```

± The "ART" of Feeding ±
°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°°
Homer Simpson likes Beer and Doughnuts, I like Curries and Bacon Butties; and
Capsule monsters like sharper foods such as swords and spears; we all LIKE to
scran FOOD; but getting back onto the Lufia topic; why the hell would Capsule
Monsters give a toss! SIMPLE! You need to Feed your monsters to evolve them; so
to begin with, let's explain things in more detail. Give a monster the foods of
the Peasants menu, and he or she will be smiling; and will be grateful for a
bowl of rice or a slice of bread; BUT feed you monsters an expensive item such
as the "Gades Blade" and you will have bullet holes in your purse, because the
little buggers will DEMAND the items of the Upper-Class menu...SO! What do we
do? thats a good question, we feed them the bargain items...How do we know how
much to feed a monster? another good question, we take a look at something on
the status screen called a FEEDING BAR!
```

Your Capsule Feeding Bar has 32 spaces; each item donated will knock of some of those spaces; these items will be worth less as a monster evolves and some will be worthless, making them fit for selling and not for eating. The rule is valid for all but ONE item in Lufia 2; the item is known as a "MAGIC FRUIT" and this special item will eliminate EIGHT spaces on the feeding bar...making your capsule monsters evolve MUCH faster. Whenever a monster is FED; a response will be given, these will be determined by the number of spaces eliminated.

0 Space(s) = Ughh - Yuk! 1 Space(s) = Mmmm - Good! 2 Space(s) = Yummy! 4 Space(s) = Yummy, Yummy! 6 Space(s) = Thank you!! 8 Space(s) = Incredible!

SO, as you can see, monsters have certain moods towards their food, but that is only half of the equation, monsters appear to request a certain weapon or armor once they've been fed ONCE; someone called RedEyedBlckFlame introduced a little information which could be quite helpful on this subject, he said that capsule monsters do appreciate a weapon known as the SILVER ROD...it's strange and it's true in most respects; all capsule monsters who are fed EXPENSIVE items will be happy to take in a SILVER ROD for their food; there's something in this I have the feeling their's a special kind of menu for each monster. I had Jelze and Blaze in their Level 2 Forms; no bars were eliminated on each of them and both of them were fed an item known as the JUTE HELMET. I got two DIFFERENT requests from the monsters, Jelze wanted a SIZZLE SWORD and Blaze requested a SECRET FRUIT...which turned out to be rather strange; but I then fed Jelze and Blaze an item known as the SCIMITAR; and for some reason, both monsters requested a not so cool DEADLY SWORD as their next meal...and this backs up the theories of RedEyedBlckFlame; monsters will appreciate certain weapons, the SILVER ROD and the DEADLY SWORD are two of them...I think monsters have strange effects with elemental weapons too...BUT I will NOT look into this too soon, it has no major importance after all; I suppose you like to feed monsters CHEAPLY; rather than blowing your hard earned cash on weapons and armours, so i've prepared a plan of feeding...all you need to do is BUY all the weapons recommended to you and feed them to your capsule monsters...the little buggers will be so pleased that all seven monsters will grow strong in an instance!

@ Changing to a monsters SECOND form!

Warp to Sundletan, the second town of the game and purchase 224 Battle knives for 44,800 Gold coins, this is near enough cash to purchase a ZIRCO ROD in the two of Narvick; each monster should be fed a total of 32 battle knives to get rid of their bars, when each monster is stuffed with all 32 bars eliminated; the monsters will evolve into their SECOND forms.

@ Changing to a monsters THIRD form!

Within the town of Sundletan, you will come across a standard version of a BACK STREET Café...beware of the drunks and purchase a whopping 224 Apple Ciders and allow you monsters to guzzle 32 of them each, after getting their stomachs pumped, all of your monsters will be in their THIRD forms; and new attacks will be learned depending on the monster. The COST of this, would be a cool 224,000 Gold Coins, because Apple Ciders are 1,000 Gold Coins per ration.

@ Changing to a monsters FORTH form!

This time Gruberik's on the cards...you need to be in the weapon store and you need 77 War Rapiers and 210 shrieks, these are essential for knocking down the monsters feeding requirements. Feed a monster one of those War Rapiers and give him/her 3 shrieks so he/she goes back to demanding War Rapiers again...you can now eliminate three more bars....so keep repeating this feeding plan and all 7 monsters can be evolved into their FORTH forms!

@ Oh Crap! How much cash did I Spend?

You have burned a HOLE in your purse, the exchange rate for a class of vigorous capsule monsters was 855,050 Gold coins, if you had used Bronze Swords for the THIRD to FORTH form evolution, you would have handed over 1,388,800 in cash; I would call such a total "PAINFUL" and we have no reason to suffer such PAIN!

@ MASTER FORMS and the fruits of Evolution!

After feeding your monsters a whole hoard of expensive items such as Apple Ciders and War Rapiers, you will be in a financial hole...and because of this the game has provided an EASIER way to make your capsule monsters even harder than before; these buggers cannot be fed WEAPONS and ITEMS, and their feeding habits change due to their elemental types. SO! Here's the deal....go see the EGG DRAGON while holding EIGHT Dragon eggs; and ask him for the fruits, he is going to present you with a grand total of 160 Fruits, 20 of each, and you will be surprised when I tell you this...A monster needs ONE of these magical fruits to evolve into their master form....In the Table below I have used three column headings, one of them is called "FORM of Evolution" you can change your capsule monsters into their previous forms from 1st to 4th, and some monsters will need to anti-evolve into one of their previous forms before the fruits can be used to evolve your monsters into master forms...the "FORM of Evolution" Column will give you the name of the form required, and the "What to Feed Him/Her?" heading should tell you which fruits is need to trigger and evolution...the information provided here, is essential for capsule monster trainers...(so ASH KETCHUM is a little dead in the water here!)
