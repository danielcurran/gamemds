## 7.1. The "ART" of Feeding

Homer Simpson likes Beer and Doughnuts, I like Curries and Bacon Butties; and Capsule monsters like sharper foods such as swords and spears; we all LIKE to scran FOOD; but getting back onto the Lufia topic; why the hell would Capsule Monsters give a toss! SIMPLE! You need to Feed your monsters to evolve them; so to begin with, let's explain things in more detail. Give a monster the foods of the Peasants menu, and he or she will be smiling; and will be grateful for a bowl of rice or a slice of bread; BUT feed you monsters an expensive item such as the "Gades Blade" and you will have bullet holes in your purse, because the little buggers will DEMAND the items of the Upper-Class menu...SO! What do we do? thats a good question, we feed them the bargain items...How do we know how much to feed a monster? another good question, we take a look at something on the status screen called a FEEDING BAR!

Your Capsule Feeding Bar has 32 spaces; each item donated will knock of some of those spaces; these items will be worth less as a monster evolves and some will be worthless, making them fit for selling and not for eating. The rule is valid for all but ONE item in Lufia 2; the item is known as a "MAGIC FRUIT" and this special item will eliminate EIGHT spaces on the feeding bar...making your capsule monsters evolve MUCH faster. Whenever a monster is FED; a response will be given, these will be determined by the number of spaces eliminated.

0 Space(s) = Ughh - Yuk! 1 Space(s) = Mmmm - Good! 2 Space(s) = Yummy! 4 Space(s) = Yummy, Yummy! 6 Space(s) = Thank you!! 8 Space(s) = Incredible!

SO, as you can see, monsters have certain moods towards their food, but that is only half of the equation, monsters appear to request a certain weapon or armor once they've been fed ONCE; someone called RedEyedBlckFlame introduced a little information which could be quite helpful on this subject, he said that capsule monsters do appreciate a weapon known as the SILVER ROD...it's strange and it's true in most respects; all capsule monsters who are fed EXPENSIVE items will be happy to take in a SILVER ROD for their food; there's something in this I have the feeling their's a special kind of menu for each monster. I had Jelze and Blaze in their Level 2 Forms; no bars were eliminated on each of them and both of them were fed an item known as the JUTE HELMET. I got two DIFFERENT requests from the monsters, Jelze wanted a SIZZLE SWORD and Blaze requested a SECRET FRUIT...which turned out to be rather strange; but I then fed Jelze and Blaze an item known as the SCIMITAR; and for some reason, both monsters requested a not so cool DEADLY SWORD as their next meal...and this backs up the theories of RedEyedBlckFlame; monsters will appreciate certain weapons, the SILVER ROD and the DEADLY SWORD are two of them...I think monsters have strange effects with elemental weapons too...BUT I will NOT look into this too soon, it has no major importance after all; I suppose you like to feed monsters CHEAPLY; rather than blowing your hard earned cash on weapons and armours, so i've prepared a plan of feeding...all you need to do is BUY all the weapons recommended to you and feed them to your capsule monsters...the little buggers will be so pleased that all seven monsters will grow strong in an instance!

@ Changing to a monsters SECOND form!

Warp to Sundletan, the second town of the game and purchase 224 Battle knives for 44,800 Gold coins, this is near enough cash to purchase a ZIRCO ROD in the two of Narvick; each monster should be fed a total of 32 battle knives to get rid of their bars, when each monster is stuffed with all 32 bars eliminated; the monsters will evolve into their SECOND forms.

@ Changing to a monsters THIRD form!

Within the town of Sundletan, you will come across a standard version of a BACK STREET Café...beware of the drunks and purchase a whopping 224 Apple Ciders and allow you monsters to guzzle 32 of them each, after getting their stomachs pumped, all of your monsters will be in their THIRD forms; and new attacks will be learned depending on the monster. The COST of this, would be a cool 224,000 Gold Coins, because Apple Ciders are 1,000 Gold Coins per ration.

@ Changing to a monsters FORTH form!

This time Gruberik's on the cards...you need to be in the weapon store and you need 77 War Rapiers and 210 shrieks, these are essential for knocking down the monsters feeding requirements. Feed a monster one of those War Rapiers and give him/her 3 shrieks so he/she goes back to demanding War Rapiers again...you can now eliminate three more bars....so keep repeating this feeding plan and all 7 monsters can be evolved into their FORTH forms!

@ Oh Crap! How much cash did I Spend?

You have burned a HOLE in your purse, the exchange rate for a class of vigorous capsule monsters was 855,050 Gold coins, if you had used Bronze Swords for the THIRD to FORTH form evolution, you would have handed over 1,388,800 in cash; I would call such a total "PAINFUL" and we have no reason to suffer such PAIN!

@ MASTER FORMS and the fruits of Evolution!

After feeding your monsters a whole hoard of expensive items such as Apple Ciders and War Rapiers, you will be in a financial hole...and because of this the game has provided an EASIER way to make your capsule monsters even harder than before; these buggers cannot be fed WEAPONS and ITEMS, and their feeding habits change due to their elemental types. SO! Here's the deal....go see the EGG DRAGON while holding EIGHT Dragon eggs; and ask him for the fruits, he is going to present you with a grand total of 160 Fruits, 20 of each, and you will be surprised when I tell you this...A monster needs ONE of these magical fruits to evolve into their master form....In the Table below I have used three column headings, one of them is called "FORM of Evolution" you can change your capsule monsters into their previous forms from 1st to 4th, and some monsters will need to anti-evolve into one of their previous forms before the fruits can be used to evolve your monsters into master forms...the "FORM of Evolution" Column will give you the name of the form required, and the "What to Feed Him/Her?" heading should tell you which fruits is need to trigger and evolution...the information provided here, is essential for capsule monster trainers...(so ASH KETCHUM is a little dead in the water here!)
